The Development History of Minecraft

The Creator: Markus "Notch" Persson

Markus Persson, known as Notch, is the Swedish programmer behind Minecraft. Born in 1979, he began coding at age 7 on his father’s Commodore 128. Inspired by open-ended games like Dwarf Fortress and Infiniminer, he started working on Minecraft in May 2009 while employed at Jalbum, a photo-sharing platform. The project quickly gained attention for its procedural world generation and complete player freedom.

The Birth and Rise of Mojang

Following Minecraft’s surprising early success in its alpha stage, Notch left Jalbum and co-founded Mojang AB in 2010 with Carl Manneh and Jakob Porsér. The small Swedish studio rapidly gained worldwide recognition, as the game’s popularity soared thanks to YouTube videos, Twitch streams, and its unique sandbox concept. Minecraft reached 1 million registered players within a year of release.

Key Milestones

  • May 17, 2009 — First public version (Pre-Classic).
  • June 28, 2010 — Beta release, adding new biomes, Nether, and multiplayer improvements.
  • November 18, 2011 — Official release at Minecon Las Vegas with version 1.0.
  • September 15, 2014 — Microsoft acquires Mojang for $2.5 billion, ensuring cross-platform support and new spin-off titles.
  • 2020–2024 — Major updates like "The Nether Update", "Caves & Cliffs", "The Wild Update", and "Trails & Tales".

Minecraft Today: Studio & Evolution

After the acquisition, Mojang became Mojang Studios in 2020, operating under Xbox Game Studios. New branches and teams were established to develop Minecraft spin-offs like Dungeons and Legends, while the core Java and Bedrock editions continue to receive massive updates. The studio now has over 100 developers and artists worldwide.

Fun Fact

Although Notch sold Mojang to Microsoft, his name still occasionally appears as an Easter egg in Minecraft splash texts!
