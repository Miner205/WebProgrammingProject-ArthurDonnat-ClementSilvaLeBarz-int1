Greatest Community Achievements

Open Library Project

This incredible project allows players around the world to access censored and banned news articles through a massive in-game library. The initiative, developed by Reporters Without Borders, uses Minecraft to bypass online censorship in certain countries. Each room of this virtual library contains texts from journalists whose work is restricted in their own nations, offering a unique example of Minecraft’s power as a tool for information freedom.

Climate Change Awareness Projects

Several community-driven projects use Minecraft to model the effects of climate change on cities, forests, and coastlines. Players can witness rising sea levels, deforestation, and natural disasters through immersive in-game experiences. These maps are often used in educational programs to raise awareness and promote eco-friendly practices among younger audiences.

Recreating Famous Landmarks

From the entire continent of Westeros (Game of Thrones) to real-world monuments like the Eiffel Tower and the Great Wall of China, Minecraft builders have pushed creative boundaries. These builds take months or even years to complete, often involving massive teams and advanced building techniques. It showcases the collaborative spirit and artistic ambition within the community.

In-Game Computers and Redstone Machines

Players have engineered fully functional computers, calculators, and even arcade games using only in-game Redstone circuits. These impressive feats replicate real-life electronic logic gates inside Minecraft’s sandbox, proving how versatile the game’s mechanics can be for simulating technology. Some of these projects can perform complex mathematical operations or even run simple programs within the game itself.
